About Le Garuda.

Team Le Garuda is the SAE Aero team from Mahindra Ecole centrale formed in the August of 2018 to take part in the SAE Aero Design challenge 2019 being held on July 4-6th. The team is to take part in the regular class section of the competition. The main aim of this competition is to build a UAV that is able to lift the maximum payload under given constrains. Our team has been able to come up with a design and now are in our final phase which is manufacturing of the aeroplane. Since this is our first time there is a lot we are learning as and when it comes.


About The Competition.

The purpose of the SAEISS Aero Design Challenge is to promote and develop Indian expertise and experience in unmanned systems technologies at the university and college levels. Even small scale unmanned vehicles are complex systems requiring a well planned and executed design approach. The competition is intended to provide undergraduate and graduate engineering students with a real-life engineering challenge. It has been designed to provide exposure to the kinds of situations that engineers face in their real-life work environment. Each team is required to conceive, design and develop a prototype of fixed wing UAV meeting the mission requirements.


The Wing Team.

The wing teams initial goal was to make select an aerofoils that would give us the maximum lift and we went for the Selig 1223. Upon running calculations for required Thrust loading and wing loading, we decided our Wing span would be around 2m and the chord length around 0.250 m. The next challenge the wing team faced was to actually make the physical model of the wing, which was initially made with Balsa wood and ABS plastic. But since our UAV must lift around 8-10 kilos we decided we should change our wing manufacturing technique to make it stronger, and hence we made it out of Aluminium, ABS plastic and balsa wood and also Ply wood.

The Tech Team.

The goal of the electronics team was to put together a radio controlled system that granted control to the ailerons, rudder, and throttle. We were also in charge for picking out the servos for the ailerons and rudder, and the motor for the propeller. We decided on the TowerPro SG90s series for our servos, and the Leopard LC5055 BLDC Outrunner motor for our propeller. In addition to this, we selected the FS iSky transmitter/receiver controller for our radio control. To further increase the stability of the airplane, we have also implemented stability augmentation through ardupilot, using the apm 2.8 board and sensors to keep track of and control various flight parameters.
